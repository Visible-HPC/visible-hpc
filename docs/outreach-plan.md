# Outreach

## Background

A number of projects have build small-scale HPC clusters for the purposes of demonstration and training.
Common demos tend to showcase the scalability of computation across nodes that HPC allows.
The domains from which these examples often come from are:

- climate modelling: coastal defences ([waves from Wee Archie](https://github.com/EPCCed/wee_archie/tree/master/waves))
- manufacturing: aeroplane wing simulation ([windtunnel from Wee Archie](https://github.com/EPCCed/wee_archie/tree/master/windtunnel))
- genomics: [AlphaFold](https://github.com/google-deepmind/alphafold)
- [image sharpening](https://train.rse.ox.ac.uk/material/HPCu/high_performance_computing/supercomputing/04_practical)
- [parallelising mandelbrot set generation](https://train.rse.ox.ac.uk/material/HPCu/high_performance_computing/parallel_computing/04_practical)

We could reuse some of those demos or work on different demos from similar fields:

- game of life: https://realpython.com/conway-game-of-life-python/
- ecology/evolution simulations: [hmsc-hpc package](https://github.com/hmsc-r/hmsc-hpc)
- spread of disease (could be scary, like [COVID](https://www.hpccoe.eu/2021/03/19/hidalgo-assisting-decision-makers-to-solve-global-challenges-with-hpc-applications-covid-19-modelling/), or historic, like the black plague)

A research domain that doesn't often make use of HPC capabilities is humanities and social sciences.
However, there are plenty of use cases for HPC in the humanities and social sciences, as the data in these disciplines are often really large and unstructured.

Below are some fairly random but fun things we could play around with, a couple of which come from research at Durham:

- language evolution (e.g. text mining to track the evolution of the context and frequency of words that are now slang terms)
- sentiment analysis (e.g. analyse the text of political speeches and track the change of emotional charge over the years)
- recursive bayesian networks: https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2021/file/22fb0cee7e1f3bde58293de743871417-Paper.pdf
- music & emotion: https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12652-024-04811-0?fromPaywallRec=false
- computational stylometry: https://programminghistorian.org/en/lessons/introduction-to-stylometry-with-python
